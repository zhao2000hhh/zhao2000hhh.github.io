Base models for complex layout generation often fail when forced to predict in a single step. SEGA decomposes layout generation into two stages — coarse prediction followed by fine-grained self-correction. Through targeted data organization, the model gains the ability to evaluate and self-correct its current layout, improving the quality of complex element placement.

Accepted to ICCV 2025 as a Highlight. Student first author, corresponding author.
