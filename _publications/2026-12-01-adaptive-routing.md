Visual generation and editing systems typically use a fixed inference budget, making it hard to balance quality and efficiency. We study adaptive inference-resource allocation for visual generation, modeling budget selection as an **ordinal decision problem** that learns the minimal sufficient compute for each sample and performs hierarchical routing accordingly — lowering average inference cost while maintaining generation quality.

Work in progress. First author.
