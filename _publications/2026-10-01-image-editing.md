Image editing models frequently fail in complex real-world scenes. Instead of modifying the backbone model, we propose a paradigm centered on **input reformulation**. By analyzing failure modes — small targets, background coupling, instruction ambiguity — we adaptively select strategies such as instruction rewriting, spatial isolation, and geometric reconstruction to transform the task into a form the model can handle. Combined with multi-step **agentic execution**, the method achieves stable gains across multiple benchmarks and models.
